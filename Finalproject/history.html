A long time ago..

My love for the mysterious, whimsical and magical world began at the tender age of six years old. On a cool Autumn night, my mother took me on an adventure. We went house to house in our neighborhood. The neighborhood had been transformed into nightmarish scene.

There were people were everywhere, dressed as a spectacular array of characters. My little heart was pounding, and I was smiling from ear to ear. Shortly, I had a bounty of sugary delights stored securely within my orange glowing pumpkin bucket. The crescendo of the evening happened on our return home.

There was one final home to pass. This home was dark. There were no adornments in the yard. Only a single sign was at the edge of the road. The sign said “Garage”. I looked down the dark driveway to see that garage door was up. I was scared. I looked at mother and said, “Let's go home”. She smiled and pointed me toward my impending doom.

As I approached, shapes began to form in the pitch-black void. A shadowy figure was sitting on a lawn chair. A wooden table was positioned next to this figure. On top of the table was a giant bowl full of large candy bars. I froze.

My stare was intense as studied this figure. It looked like a giant stuffed wolf. The wolf didn’t move. I gazed upon the wolf for what seemed to be an eternity looking for any signs of movement. The wolf didn’t move. I took a step forward. The wolf didn’t move. I took three steps forward. The wolf didn’t move.

I had now committed to the mission. Those candys were mine. I was determined. I threw my hands up in a final act bravery and scream at the wolf. The wolf didn’t move.

I broke into a sprint toward the garage. My eyes were fixated on the bowl of candy. I reached forward to retrieve my reward as my feet crossed the threshold. A burst of light erupted as I entered the garage. I stopped. The light was flashing so fast. I was disoriented. I turned my gaze from the candy to the wolf. The wolf moved.

I was mesmerized, frightened, excited and delighted as I experienced my first Halloween. The scene in the garage was the first time I had ever experienced a strobe light. I was in awe as my neighbor moved within the light looking like a real life stop animation werewolf. My fear left and pure joy came over me.

This event sparked my imagination. I have a passion for immersive experiences. Creating experiences should be a done in a way that leads to joy. Simple elements such as storytelling, pacing, setting and atmosphere lead users to become invested in discovering more. I enjoy creating stories and experiences. I am most happy when allowed to let my imagination run free.
