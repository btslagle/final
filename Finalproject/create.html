Creative

My creativity comes in many forms. I enjoy writing, music, inventing and problems solving. I am most happy and alive when I am in creative flow. I want to demonstrate my creative process with a concept for a haunted house attraction that I have created called “Corridors”. My love for the mysterious, whimsical and spooky begain an early age. Learn more here.

First, I thought of a core concept. Fear comes in my forms. I chose the image of a closed door with light emanating from the cracks around the door. This image symbolizes a world of possibilities of what could be beyond the door.

A door is a barrier. A door can serve to keep someone out or to contain something within. A Corridor is long passage filled with doors and possibilities. Be careful which doors you chose to open.

I decided that I wanted the layout of the attraction to be a reocurring set of corridors. I made a rough sketch to define the pathway around the haunt.

I sectioned it into a staging area, themed rooms and mazes that would lead between the themed rooms. Upon entry, guests are greeted in the staging at the beginning of a long open hallway that ends in at a set of elevator doors. A long the wall of the hallway are numerous doors that lead to dead ends, frights and the entrance to the next themed section.

This staging area is the key element to the attraction. It will be recreated three times throughout the haunt. Everytime the guest finsh a themed area they walk into another exact copy of the stagging area as if they were first entering the attraction. This creates a feeling of a loop they can not escape.

Next, I created the themed sections of the attraction. The purpose of the themed section is to show what happens when you chose to enter the wrong door.

The first theme was that of late 1800s hotel based upon the true story of H.H. Holmes. Holmes built hotel rooms in to traps that executed his victims.

The second theme was a hospital theme based upon the true story of Charles Cullen a nurse that murdered up to 40 of his patients.

The final section of the haunt is a twisted reality escape from the attraction. This section of the haunt will incorporate illusions, obstacles, props and blacklight paint. The idea is to have a disorienting path and create a since of urgency to escape.

The attraction ends back in the stagging area. The evelvator doors are open with an exit sign glowing inside. The final scene is a giant monster charging the guest from the elevator.
